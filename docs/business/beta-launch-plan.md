# SpecForge — Beta Launch Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how to acquire the first 10–20 meaningful beta users for SpecForge without requiring an existing audience or large budget.

## Beta Goal

Find the first 10–20 users who genuinely feel the problem SpecForge solves and can provide high-quality feedback.

This phase prioritizes signal quality over top-of-funnel volume.

## Budget Assumption

Estimated available budget: $200–$500.

## Channel 1 — Community Seeding

### Strategy

Participate genuinely in high-signal founder, SaaS, PM, and entrepreneur communities where people already discuss customer discovery and product prioritization problems.

### Candidate Communities

- Reddit:
  - r/startups
  - r/SaaS
  - r/ProductManagement
  - r/Entrepreneur
- Indie Hackers
- relevant Slack or Discord communities

### Operating Principle

Do not drop cold links immediately. First add value:

- answer questions
- share synthesis or discovery insights
- engage in relevant threads
- mention SpecForge naturally when it directly solves the problem being discussed

### Timing

Spend the first week building credibility before any formal push.

## Channel 2 — Product Hunt Launch

### Strategy

Launch with a clear product page and demo flow.

### Launch Assets

- one-line tagline
- short description
- attention-grabbing demo GIF
- screenshots showing upload → analyze → export
- a small number of advance supporters

### Timing Recommendation

Launch on a Tuesday or Wednesday for stronger visibility.

### Expected Outcome

A strong launch can generate meaningful traffic even without an established personal brand if the product page and demo are clear.

## Channel 3 — Newsletter Sponsorship

### Strategy

Use most of the paid budget on one highly targeted newsletter placement rather than broad paid social ads.

### Candidate Newsletters

- TLDR Product
- Indie Hackers Newsletter
- Pragmatic Engineer
- Lenny’s Newsletter

### Budget Guidance

- primary spend: approximately $200–$300
- hold remaining budget for a second placement if the first performs well

### Rationale

A relevant niche audience is more valuable than broad but low-intent traffic.

## Channel 4 — Waitlist Before Launch

### Strategy

Set up a simple landing page with a waitlist before launch.

### Benefits

- captures intent before launch
- builds an email list for launch day
- improves social proof
- lets all community and newsletter traffic point somewhere focused

### Recommended Headline

**AI turns your customer feedback into build-ready feature specs — automatically.**

### Recommended Subheadline

Upload your interviews and feedback. Our AI synthesizes the insights and generates structured specs ready for Cursor or Claude Code.

## Channel 5 — Personal Brand (Optional)

### Strategy

If the founder is comfortable being public, use personal distribution to amplify awareness.

### Tactics

- build-in-public posts on X/Twitter
- LinkedIn posts about the problem and solution
- short demo videos on YouTube or Loom

### Principle

Talk about the problem space as much as the product.

## Budget Allocation Recommendation

Suggested allocation:

- $0 on broad paid social
- $200–$300 on a targeted newsletter sponsorship
- $0–$100 on optional tools or creative needs
- reserve remaining budget for a second newsletter test if justified

## Key Principle

The goal is not scale. The goal is finding early users who deeply care about the problem and will provide useful feedback.

One engaged beta user is worth far more than many signups who never activate.

## Launch Readiness Checklist

Before beta launch:

- landing page live
- waitlist active
- demo flow polished
- upload → analyze → export path working
- messaging clear
- beta feedback collection plan ready
