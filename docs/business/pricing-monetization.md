# SpecForge — Pricing & Monetization

## Purpose

This document captures the planned pricing strategy and monetization rollout outside the MVP implementation scope.

## Philosophy

SpecForge should not charge from day one. Pricing should be introduced based on validated usage milestones rather than time alone. Early product learning is more important than early revenue.

Charging too early risks suppressing honest feedback and reducing adoption from the early users who matter most.

## Rollout Thresholds

### 0–100 Users

- fully free
- no credit card required
- full product experience available
- goal is validation and feedback, not revenue

### 100–500 Users

- introduce a soft freemium rollout
- grandfather early adopters into Pro for 30 days
- new users land on the Free tier
- begin testing upgrade triggers

### 500+ Users

- activate full pricing model
- promote team tier
- begin treating MRR as a core business metric

## Pricing Model

Freemium is preferred over a time-limited free trial.

### Why Freemium

- supports habit formation
- reduces friction during adoption
- aligns with bottoms-up product usage
- creates more natural upgrade moments than an expiring trial

## Planned Tiers

## Free — $0/month

### Who It’s For

Solo founders and light users validating fit.

### Included

- 2 projects per month
- up to 5 files per project
- AI analysis and proposal generation
- Markdown export up to 3 proposals per project
- no long-term session persistence
- projects expire after 7 days

## Pro — $29/month

### Who It’s For

Active PMs and founders using SpecForge regularly.

### Included

- unlimited projects
- up to 20 files per project
- full proposal export
- indefinite session persistence
- re-run analysis after adding feedback
- priority AI processing

## Team — $79/month

### Who It’s For

Small teams needing shared access and collaboration.

### Included

- everything in Pro
- up to 5 team members
- shared workspaces and projects
- commenting and annotation
- admin controls and role-based access

## Pricing Rationale

- $29 is positioned as an easy individual spend.
- $79 is priced for lightweight team adoption without procurement friction.
- Free should be useful enough to build habit, but limited enough to create upgrade triggers.

## Key Metrics to Watch

When pricing goes live, track:

- Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR)
- free-to-Pro conversion rate
- upgrade trigger usage
- persistence-related conversion behavior

Healthy benchmark target from the source strategy:

- B2B freemium conversion rate around 3–5%

If conversion is below 2%, revisit:

- Free tier generosity
- Pro value proposition
- upgrade trigger design

## Not for MVP Build Scope

The following are business-planning items, not MVP implementation requirements unless re-scoped:

- feature gating
- billing integration
- paid checkout
- team-seat billing
- monetization analytics dashboard
